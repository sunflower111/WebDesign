Foxes

Red Foxes

Red foxes live in many diverse habitats including forests, grasslands, mountains, and deserts. They can also live in human environments. Red foxes hunt alone and feed on rodents, rabbits, birds, and other small game, but they will also eat fruit and vegetables, fish, frogs, and even worms. The fox's tail helps it balance and keep warm. It's also used as a signal flag to communicate with other foxes. They also communicate with making scent posts. Click the image for more information.

Gray Foxes

Gray foxes have mostly gray fur along with red and white fur. They have strong, hooked claws that let them climb trees and avoid predators. They also have a black stripe on their tail. They live throughout most rocky, wooded, brushy regions of North America. They are omnivorous and have similar diets to red foxes. Click the image for more information.
